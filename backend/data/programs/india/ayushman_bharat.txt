Program Name: Ayushman Bharat Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (AB-PMJAY)

Overview:
Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY provides cashless hospital coverage for eligible low-income and vulnerable families in India. Coverage is used for secondary and tertiary care hospitalization at empaneled public and private hospitals.

Target Demographic:
Economically vulnerable Indian households identified through deprivation and occupational criteria, including rural households with housing deprivation, manual casual labor households, landless laborers, Scheduled Caste or Scheduled Tribe households, and listed urban occupational categories.

Income and Asset Thresholds:
The MVP rule treats low-income households with annual household income up to Rs 2.5 lakh as a strong signal, with extra weight for ration card status, SECC vulnerability, manual labor, disability, homelessness, or lack of durable assets. Ownership of high-value assets, formal-sector high income, or private comprehensive health insurance are negative signals.

Benefit Value:
Hardcoded annual health insurance coverage for MVP clock math: up to Rs 5,00,000 per family per year. Because this is coverage rather than cash, use a conservative monthly realized value estimate of Rs 2,000. Use USD 24 per month as the approximate monthly value.

Eligibility Signals:
Strong signals include India country selection, low-income household, hospitalization risk, ration card or deprivation category, and no existing comprehensive insurance. Negative signals include high income, high-value asset ownership, or already having equivalent insurance.
