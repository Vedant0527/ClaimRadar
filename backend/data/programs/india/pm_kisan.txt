PROGRAM OVERVIEW:
Name: PM Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN).
Administering agency: Department of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare, Government of India.
Official URL: https://pmkisan.gov.in/
PM-KISAN gives direct income support to eligible farmer families for agricultural and household needs.

ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA:
Applicant household must be a farmer family with cultivable land recorded in official land records. Dummy MVP rule: small or marginal farmer with land holding up to 2 hectares is a strong match. Husband, wife, and minor children count as one family unit. Exclusions: institutional landholders, current or former constitutional post holders, current or former ministers, mayors, district panchayat chairpersons, serving or retired government officers except allowed Class IV/multitasking staff, income tax payers, doctors, engineers, lawyers, chartered accountants, architects registered with professional bodies, and pensioners receiving more than ₹10,000 per month except allowed categories.

BENEFIT AMOUNTS:
Exact dummy program value: ₹6,000 per year paid in three installments of ₹2,000 each. Monthly equivalent for clock math: ₹500 per month. Payment is direct benefit transfer to beneficiary bank account after land, Aadhaar, and bank validation.

APPLICATION PROCESS:
Register through PM-KISAN portal, Common Service Centre, or state agriculture office. Provide Aadhaar, mobile number, bank account, land record details, ration card where requested, and self-declaration. Complete eKYC, correct land-seeding issues, and monitor beneficiary status. State nodal officer verifies record before central payment approval.

KEY DATES:
Enrollment is generally open year-round. Installments are normally released every four months. Beneficiaries should complete eKYC and land record correction before installment processing cutoffs announced by government.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS:
Q1: Does tenant farmer qualify?
A1: Dummy rule says no unless cultivable land is recorded in applicant family name.
Q2: Can both husband and wife receive benefit separately?
A2: No. Family unit receives one benefit.
Q3: Is eKYC required?
A3: Yes, eKYC is commonly required for continued payment.
Q4: What if land record spelling differs from Aadhaar?
A4: Applicant should correct demographic or land record mismatch before approval.
Q5: Are income tax payers eligible?
A5: No. Income tax payer status is exclusion signal.
